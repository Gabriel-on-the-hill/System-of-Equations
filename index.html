Before You Begin: The Big 3 Strategies

1. Desmos (The Primary Weapon)

Type both equations exactly as they appear. Click the intersection point to find $(x, y)$. This solves the vast majority of systems on the SAT.

2. Substitution (The Sneaky Shortcut)

If $y = 3x$ and $2x + y = 10$, swap $y$ for $3x$: $2x + 3x = 10$. Best when one variable is already alone on one side.

3. Elimination (The Stack Attack)

Stack equations vertically. Add or subtract down the columns to kill a variable. Best for equations in $Ax + By = C$ form.
